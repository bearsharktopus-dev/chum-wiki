    Morris "Pop-Pop Moe" Small¶

    Sam Small's paternal grandfather, a retired civil engineer and co-founder of Horvath-Small Ltd, whose past work on Daedalus Correctional Facility haunts his legacy even as he serves as one of Sam's most important moral guides.

    Background¶

    Morris "Pop-Pop Moe" Small is Sam Small's paternal grandfather, a retired civil engineer who co-founded Horvath-Small Ltd. with his business partner John Horvath. The firm specialized in weatherproofing and environmental engineering, and over the course of Morris's career, it completed infrastructure projects throughout the northeastern United States. Among its most significant contracts was the weather and disaster-proofing work on Daedalus Correctional Facility, the nation's first purpose-built superhuman prison, which became operational in 2006. Morris's involvement with Daedalus would later become a source of profound personal regret.

    Morris was present during the boating accident that activated Sam's powers in the summer of 2023. He later revealed to Sam and her cousin Abigail that Sam's transformation was far more violent than Sam remembered or was told -- during the accident, Sam destroyed the boat's engine and became what Morris described as a "snarling beast of the sea, like an angry marlin." He apologized to Sam for the accident during their first on-page interaction and subsequently became one of the earliest figures to subtly encourage Sam's superhero activities, telling her at a Shabbat dinner shortly after her JLUMA was issued that the purpose of her abilities was simple: to save people's lives.

    Morris is a widower. His wife Leah passed away prior to the events of the story, and he retired to a three-story brick rowhome in Ventnor, New Jersey, where he finds peace by the ocean. He took Sam to visit Leah's grave at Mount Zion Cemetery in New York, where Sam learned she was named after her great-great-grandmother Sofia and her great-grandfather Elijah. Morris's mother-in-law (Leah's mother) was a Holocaust survivor, an experience that profoundly shaped Morris's worldview and his philosophy about self-reliance and the responsible use of power. He later taught his son Benjamin to shoot at age 21, imparting the lesson that sometimes there would be no superhero around and sometimes there would just be you.

    Prior to the events of the story, Morris met with Liberty Belle on multiple occasions regarding Daedalus Correctional Facility. Belle visited Morris at his home in 2014 to discuss how the facility had evolved from containment to deliberate isolation, showing him photographs of isolation cells and their psychological effects on inmates. Morris admitted to having filed anonymous concerns about ventilation and long-term effects during construction but acknowledged he had not taken stronger action. These meetings connected Morris to the broader conspiracy surrounding Daedalus and the mysterious figure known as Porcelain, whom Morris had encountered at the construction site under the alias "Espinosa."

    Training & Skills¶

    • Civil Engineering Co-founded and operated Horvath-Small Ltd., a firm recognized for weatherproofing and environmental engineering. Completed major infrastructure projects across the northeastern United States, including work on the Verrazano-Narrows Bridge and Daedalus Correctional Facility.
    • Firearms Proficiency Taught his son Benjamin to shoot. Preferred teaching in the woods with watermelons rather than at formal ranges. His philosophy on firearms and power was pragmatic: power of any kind is neither good nor bad, and what matters is when, why, and how it is used.
    • Storytelling and Moral Instruction Possesses a deep knowledge of Jewish parables, religious teachings, family history, and comic book narratives, which he draws upon to guide Sam and other family members through moral and ethical dilemmas.
    • Institutional Knowledge of Daedalus Retains detailed engineering knowledge of Daedalus Correctional Facility's construction, including its design specifications, contractor relationships, and the modifications that transformed it from a containment facility into an instrument of isolation.

    Personality¶

    Morris is philosophical, warm, and quietly authoritative. He speaks with a mix of gentle humor and earned wisdom, frequently drawing upon Jewish parables, family history, and stories from comic books and science fiction to frame the moral lessons he imparts. He explained kosher dietary laws to Sam as a metaphor for the balance between knowledge and action -- ruminant animals chew cud (reflection) and have split hooves (action), and a person needs both. During Rosh Hashanah, he served as the Maggid, recounting the Exodus narrative, and later offered philosophical commentary on free will and the consequences of choices. When Sam faced the growing threat of Pattinson's Pals and social upheaval, Morris gave her a copy of "Green Lantern/Green Arrow: Hard Traveling Heroes," a comic about superheroes confronting social injustice.

    Beneath his storytelling warmth, Morris carries significant moral weight. He regrets his involvement with Daedalus and considers it a stain on his legacy, acknowledging that he did not ask enough questions when concerning modifications were made to the facility's design. He filed anonymous complaints but recognizes he could have done more. This regret does not paralyze him; instead, it informs the counsel he gives Sam, encouraging her to keep questioning difficult ethical issues rather than accepting complicity through silence. He is a source of tension with his son Benjamin, as the two disagree about Sam's superhero activities -- Morris subtly encourages them while Benjamin prioritizes her safety -- a philosophical divide that has produced at least one overheard argument at the hospital during Sam's recovery from Chernobyl.

    Character Relationship Notes
    Sam Small (Bloodhound) Granddaughter Morris is one of Sam's most important moral guides. He was present during her boating accident and later became one of the first family members to subtly encourage her superhero activities. He shares cultural, religious, and literary wisdom with her to shape her moral compass. Sam confronted him about his Daedalus involvement after discovering his name in Liberty Belle's notebooks.
    Benjamin Small Son Father and son share a loving but philosophically divided relationship. Benjamin prioritizes Sam's safety while Morris defends her right to follow her calling, leading to arguments. Morris taught Benjamin to shoot and instilled in him a philosophy of self-reliance. Benjamin's city planning work reflects Morris's engineering background.
    Rachel Small Daughter-in-law Rachel respects Morris and includes him in family gatherings and discussions. During an interlude set at the Small home, Morris, Rachel, and Ben discussed their fears about Sam's vigilante work, with Morris using a story about building the Verrazano-Narrows Bridge as a parenting metaphor. Morris tactfully mediated between Rachel and her mother Camilla when tensions arose.
    Leah Small Wife (deceased) Morris's late wife. Her passing led him to retire to Ventnor, where he finds peace by the ocean. Leah's mother was a Holocaust survivor, an experience that profoundly influenced Morris's worldview and his teachings about self-reliance and vigilance. Sam visits Leah's grave at Mount Zion Cemetery in New York.
    Abigail Silverman Granddaughter (by marriage, via niece/nephew) Sam's cousin who functions as a sister figure. Present during the Tashlich scene at Rosh Hashanah where Morris shared the story of great-grandfather Elijah and revealed details about Sam's power manifestation.
    John Horvath Business partner Co-founder of Horvath-Small Ltd. The two worked together on infrastructure projects including Daedalus. Horvath later designed the renovation blueprints for the Tacony Music Hall's conversion into the Tacony Community Center, connecting Morris's professional legacy to Sam's present-day work.
    Liberty Belle (Diane Williams) Informant / Contact Belle visited Morris multiple times to discuss Daedalus, including a 2014 meeting at his home where she showed him photographs of the facility's inhumane conditions. Morris provided information about contractors and design changes. Belle was aware of Morris's relationship to Sam.
    Uncle Shelley (Herschel Small) Son Morris's other son. During the Rosh Hashanah dinner in Arc 7, Shelley publicly revealed Morris's involvement in building Daedalus, causing shock among the younger family members.
    Schlemiel Pet Morris's cat, introduced during the Small family's Shabbat dinners. Named with characteristic humor.

    Trivia¶

    • Morris claims to have met Stan Lee and Steve Ditko, as well as Mr. Chakravarti (Mr. Genesis), whom he described as a true hero.
    • His cat Schlemiel is named with characteristic Jewish humor -- a schlemiel is a habitually unlucky or bungling person in Yiddish.
    • Morris taught Sam about kosher animals as a metaphor for moral balance: animals must both chew cud (reflect on knowledge) and have split hooves (take action). Sam would later cite this teaching in her moral reasoning.
    • He enjoys classic Jewish foods, reads Arthur Conan Doyle, and loves comic books and science fiction. His home contains a bookshelf stuffed with yellowed comics and dog-eared pulp novels.
    • He wears a cord with a pendant of personal significance, though the specific meaning of the pendant has not been revealed.
    • The Verrazano-Narrows Bridge, which Morris referenced in his parenting metaphor, was completed in 1964, placing him in the workforce during the mid-twentieth century and consistent with his age.
    • Sam concluded early in the story that she could never be a villain because she could not bear disappointing her Pop-Pop.
    • Morris's business partner John Horvath later designed the renovation blueprints for the Tacony Community Center, directly linking Morris's professional network to Sam's community work.
    • His great-grandfather Elijah's experiences with the House Un-American Activities Committee establish a generational pattern of the Small family being targeted by government overreach, paralleling Sam's conflicts with the NSRA and anti-vigilante legislation.
    • During the Daedalus construction, Morris encountered a British contractor named Jared Cross who requested additional isolation features for "emergent conditions" (powers) and demonstrated his own tar-based powers during containment testing. An Israeli consultant named "Espinosa" observed the testing and praised Pop-pop's work; "Espinosa" was later identified as possibly being Porcelain, the leader of Red Calf and one of the story's most dangerous figures.
    • The government covered all cost overruns on Daedalus without question, a red flag Morris noted but did not act on strongly enough beyond anonymous complaints about ventilation and long-term isolation effects.
