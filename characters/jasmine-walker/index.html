    Jasmine Walker¶

    Fifteen-year-old chlorokinetic and member of the Tacony Community Center mentorship program, transferred to Philadelphia from the Whitford Institute in Chicago following a traumatic activation event that killed her father.

    Background¶

    Jasmine Walker is a teenage metahuman whose powers activated in approximately May 2025 under circumstances traumatic enough to require institutional intervention. During the activation event, Jasmine's father was killed. He had been born in 1979, placing him just outside the threshold for potential superhuman emergence (individuals born 1981 and later), meaning he could not have developed powers of his own and had no biological defenses against whatever occurred during his daughter's manifestation. Jasmine internalized her father's death as a deeply personal failing, interpreting it through what the character reference guide describes as "a sort of agnostic divine intervention thing" the cruel randomness of a system that gave her abilities she never asked for while killing the parent who could never have shared them. She does not reveal the circumstances of her father's death to others.

    Following the activation incident, Jasmine was admitted to the Whitford Institute in Chicago, the only psychiatric inpatient-outpatient facility for powered youth in the country. The Whitford intervention indicates that her early power manifestations were sufficiently alarming to require institutional containment and treatment, though not dangerous enough to warrant placement at Aurora Springs Residential Facility. At Whitford, Jasmine's subconscious plant manipulation was treated as a threat rather than a symptom, reinforcing her sense of isolation and the perception that she was dangerous. She was subsequently transferred to Philadelphia as part of a partial-stay program, where she was identified by Councilman Jamal Davis as one of four initial cases for his pilot peer mentorship program.

    Jasmine's mother holds a demanding, high-paying job that provides financial stability but leaves little time for her daughter. In Philadelphia, Jasmine resides with a foster mother who works in a medical capacity (arriving at pickups in scrubs) and who has been characterized as arriving late and with limited engagement her foster mother was the last parent to arrive during the Songbird attack on the community center, showing up forty-five minutes after the incident. Jasmine walked to the car without speaking. The combination of an absent biological mother, a deceased father, institutional upbringing, and a foster placement that appears functionally adequate but emotionally distant has left Jasmine profoundly isolated.

    Powers & Abilities¶

    PERKS Assessment¶

    No official PERKS assessment on file.

    Abilities¶

    • Chlorokinesis (Plant Manipulation) Jasmine possesses the ability to influence and direct plant matter in her vicinity. Plants nearby respond to her emotional state, particularly feelings of threat, repositioning themselves to shield her. This manifests as branches bending toward her, leaves draping protectively over her body, roots and grasses reaching in her direction, and dormant plants spontaneously sprouting. The response operates on a subconscious, automatic level tied to her emotional state.
    • Protective Plant Response When startled or frightened, nearby plants move to physically intercept perceived threats and create barriers between Jasmine and whatever she perceives as dangerous. During the Pennypack Park training session in January 2026, dead tree branches, bushes, and grass moved to catch Liam O'Connor when he slipped and fell toward her, demonstrating that even dormant or dead plant material responds to her impulses.
    • Escalation Potential (Protection to Harm) Jasmine's powers can shift from defensive ("protect me") to offensive ("hurt them") if her conscious impulse changes. During the Songbird attack on the community center, plants within the building grew thorns oriented toward the door a defensive posture that also constituted a hostile response to the perceived threat outside. Dr. Jensen flagged this escalation potential as high-risk.
    • Area-of-Effect Emotional Broadcast During states of intense fear, Jasmine's influence extends well beyond her immediate surroundings. When frightened during the Songbird attack in March 2026, hedges within one to two blocks of the community center leaned toward the building, weeds pushed through pavement, and a winter-dormant garden began spontaneously sprouting all orienting toward Jasmine's location. Sam Small noted this range as significant and undocumented.

    Limitations¶

    • Jasmine's control over her chlorokinesis is currently involuntary and subconscious. She cannot consciously direct plants and has expressed distress over the inability to stop the response once triggered ("I can't make them stop").
    • Plant material manipulated by her power retains its structural properties wood is only as strong as wood, and her ability cannot make organic material exceed its natural physical limits. However, thorns remain sharp and branches can still strike with considerable force.
    • The power creates literal physical barriers between Jasmine and other people, reinforcing her psychological isolation in a feedback loop: fear produces plant walls, plant walls push people away, being pushed away produces more fear.
    • The full upper limits of her range and the scope of plant material she can influence are not yet established, though the two-block radius observed during the Songbird attack represents the most extreme documented manifestation.

    Training & Skills¶

    • Participating in mentorship program sessions run by Sam Small and Maggie O'Brien, including group discussions about civilian encounters, de-escalation techniques, and career planning.
    • Receiving individual therapy from Dr. Hernandez at the Tacony Community Center.
    • Previously received psychiatric treatment at the Whitford Institute, though the nature and focus of that treatment beyond containment is not detailed.
    • Sam Small has committed to helping Jasmine learn conscious direction of her powers, though progress toward voluntary control has not yet been documented.

    Personality¶

    Jasmine presents a black goth aesthetic that suggests an interest in the macabre or "creepy," but her actual interests run toward conventionally beautiful things butterflies and flower arranging among them. The disconnect between presentation and preference is characteristic of her broader behavioral pattern: Jasmine constructs an exterior designed to keep people at a comfortable distance while privately craving the connection she is too afraid to pursue. She is described as the "bad shy" in contrast to Zara Khan's "good shy" where Zara is quietly curious and willing to engage on topics she knows well, Jasmine wants to be perceived as the cool, detached quiet kid in the corner. She sits back and observes, saves up cutting remarks, and performs the role of a quiet curmudgeon until she believes she has something sufficiently clever to contribute.

    Beneath the affect is a teenager who craves positive attention and affection but is scared to reach out for it. This fear is compounded by the trauma of her father's death during her activation, the institutional experience at Whitford where her powers were treated as dangerous, and a home life that provides material stability but little emotional warmth. Her chlorokinesis reinforces this cycle on a physical level the plants that move to protect her also wall her off from the people around her, creating a visible manifestation of the emotional barriers she has built. At the time of Arc 16, Jasmine is described as "very slowly defrosting," suggesting incremental progress toward trust and openness within the mentorship peer group.

    Trivia¶

    • Jasmine's powers were undisclosed for approximately two months of mentorship sessions before being involuntarily revealed at Pennypack Park, making her the last of the four mentees to have her abilities seen by the group.
    • Her father's birth year of 1979 is specifically noted as placing him just before the 1981 threshold for superhuman potential a two-year gap that Jasmine has internalized as cosmically significant.
    • The character reference guide describes Jasmine as representing "kids failed by psychiatric and institutional systems despite those systems genuinely trying to help," positioning her as a thematic counterpoint to characters like Daisy Zhen who were failed by systems that were not trying to help.
    • Jasmine's chlorokinesis creates a literalized metaphor: her power physically constructs the emotional barriers she uses to keep people away, with plants forming walls between her and others in direct response to the fear and isolation that drive her withdrawal.
    • She is one of two mentorship members (alongside Zara Khan) whom Sam directly addressed regarding the racial disparity in how the justice system treats powered youth of color.
    • During the career planning discussion with the mentorship group, Liam said he wanted to be an electrician, Zara said research, and Alex said business or drag racing. Jasmine was the only one who said she did not know.
    • The Whitford Institute, where Jasmine was previously housed, is also where Daisy Zhen was remanded following her emergency legal hearing in Arc 13. It is the only facility of its kind in the country.
    • Dr. Jensen flagged three of the four mentorship members (Jasmine, Zara, and Liam); Alex Kirby was the exception, having been referred through Davis's professional connection to his parents.
