    Lucas Donovan¶

    Founding member and co-lead interrogator of the Kingdom of Keys' Philadelphia branch, known for his lie-detection abilities and volatile temperament masked by professional composure.

    Background¶

    Lucas Donovan was born on September 15, 1985, in Trenton, New Jersey, to Victoria and Raymond Donovan. He grew up in a household characterized by dishonesty and maintained appearances, an environment that would prove formative in shaping both his temperament and his eventual relationship with his metahuman abilities. His power first manifested on June 17, 2001, during a family dinner when he experienced an itching sensation in his skull as his father lied about his whereabouts the previous night. This initial activation event tied Lucas's abilities directly to the domestic deceptions that defined his upbringing.

    After relocating to Philadelphia in 2007, Lucas worked as a bartender, quietly using his nascent lie-detection abilities to identify patrons who were cheating or running scams. By 2010, he had drifted into organized crime, joining a small-time criminal gang where he earned the nickname "Polygraph" for his uncanny ability to identify deception. His powers proved invaluable during gang operations, most notably in 2012 when he uncovered a planned double-cross that saved the group from a disastrous outcome. When the gang disbanded following a major police crackdown in 2016, Lucas freelanced his interrogation services throughout Philadelphia's criminal underworld.

    On October 31, 2019, Mr. Antithesis approached Lucas and offered him the role of lead interrogator for the newly forming Kingdom of Keys. Lucas accepted, formally adopting the designation "Mr. Polygraph" and cultivating a more professional appearance to align with the organization's corporate criminal ethos. As a founding member of the Kingdom, he became instrumental in vetting recruits, maintaining internal security, and conducting interrogations alongside his partner, Mr. Nothing (Darnell Hayes). The two operated as the Philadelphia branch's co-lead interrogators, a role that placed Lucas at the center of the Kingdom's intelligence operations for several years until his capture and depowering by Rogue Wave in late 2025.

    Powers & Abilities¶

    PERKS Assessment¶

    No official PERKS assessment on file.

    Abilities¶

    • Lie Detection (Spoken Word) Lucas possesses an extrasensory ability to determine whether someone is truthfully responding to a question he asks. When a subject speaks a falsehood, he experiences a distinct unpleasant twinge, referred to as a "ping," in the back of his skull. This ability functions regardless of physical proximity and operates over telephone calls, video calls, and any other medium that transmits spoken communication. The power detects subjective truth rather than objective reality, meaning it registers whether the speaker believes they are telling a falsehood rather than whether the statement is factually accurate. This nuance allows Lucas to identify individuals who are unknowingly spreading misinformation, as their genuine belief in the falsehood would not trigger a ping. Conversely, someone who believes they are lying but happens to state something factually true would still trigger the sensation.

    Limitations¶

    • The ability requires spoken communication to function and is completely ineffective against written statements, non-verbal cues, or text-based deception.
    • Clever wordplay, half-truths, and statements that are technically correct but misleading can circumvent the power, as the subjective belief component may not register these as falsehoods.
    • The physical manifestation of the ping as a skull sensation can become distracting or overwhelming in environments where multiple lies are being told simultaneously, requiring significant mental discipline to manage during complex interrogations.
    • Recorded playback of another person's voice does not trigger the power, as demonstrated during the Soot sting operation when the Auditors used Devonte's sound-replaying abilities to feed Kate's pre-recorded voice through Sam's mask, bypassing Lucas's detection entirely.
    • Following his capture by Rogue Wave, Snake Oil extracted Lucas's lie-detection power through direct physical contact, crystallizing it into a blue-green substance over approximately 90 seconds. This left Lucas permanently depowered. As of the beginning of Arc 16, Mr. Antithesis is actively hunting Fly to provide Lucas with a replacement power, and it is implied he has re-acquired a new ability, though its nature has not yet been revealed.

    Training & Skills¶

    • One of the best marksmen in the Kingdom of Keys among members whose powers do not enhance their aim. He is consistently described as a crack shot with firearms.
    • Excellent chess player, reflecting his strategic intelligence and capacity for anticipating opponents' moves.
    • Genuinely intelligent, capable of running complex interrogations that blend psychological pressure with his supernatural abilities.
    • Skilled in intimidation and psychological manipulation, employing threats of extreme violence (such as Yakuza-style finger removal) to extract compliance even without using his power.
    • Practiced meditator, using breathing techniques to manage his anger issues and maintain composure during high-pressure interrogations.
    • Experienced in impersonating law enforcement, having successfully posed as NSRA Agent Parker with fabricated credentials during the Hatboro-Horsham investigation.

    Personality¶

    Lucas Donovan presents as professional and controlled, maintaining a composed exterior that belies significant anger management issues simmering beneath the surface. He is impulsive in his thinking but disciplined in his actions, possessing the self-awareness to lid his volatile impulses most of the time. When that lid slips, the results are explosive: he has been observed firing his gun into machinery in frustration, breaking a subordinate's nose as a substitute for cutting off his finger, and escalating confrontations beyond what the tactical situation requires. His rage appears rooted in a deep-seated frustration with dishonesty, a trait likely exacerbated by a power that forces him to confront untruths constantly.

    In his professional capacity, Lucas is direct, authoritative, and comfortable exercising lethal force. He executed a suspected informant with two gunshots during a recruitment meeting on Halloween to silence dissent and demonstrate consequences. He assigned Aaron McKinley to kill Bloodhound as casually as delegating a work task. Yet he is not purely sadistic; he simply operates within the Kingdom's framework of criminal pragmatism, treating violence as a business tool rather than a source of personal pleasure. Outside of work, Lucas is described as someone who simply has his life and lives it, with interests in classic detective novels, jazz music, poker, and a collection of vintage polygraph machines that reflects his respect for the tradition of lie detection.

    Relationships¶

    Character Relationship Notes
    Darnell Hayes (Mr. Nothing) Partner / Close friend Co-lead interrogator and operational partner for the Philadelphia branch. The two form an inseparable duo described as "heterosexual life mates," despite having met only relatively recently through the Kingdom. They complement each other tactically: Lucas provides intelligence-gathering through lie detection while Darnell provides power nullification in the field.
    Trent Fairfax (Mr. Antithesis) Superior / Benefactor Leader of the Kingdom of Keys who recruited Lucas as a founding member in 2019. Following Lucas's depowering, Mr. Antithesis personally undertook a campaign to acquire Fly to restore Lucas's abilities, demonstrating the organization's investment in his value.
    Maya Richardson (Mrs. Zenith) Colleague Acting boss of the Philadelphia branch. Maya coordinated Lucas's legal representation following his arrest and visited him in the hospital after his depowering. She assigned him and Mr. Nothing to oversee Hypeman production coordination with Mrs. Xenograft.
    Sam Small (Bloodhound) Adversary Teenage vigilante who became Lucas's most persistent antagonist. Their encounters escalated from the Dobson Factory confrontation through the NSRA impersonation to the Soot sting, culminating in Sam orchestrating the tip that led to his capture and depowering by Rogue Wave.
    Aaron McKinley (The Coyote) Subordinate Low-level criminal whom Lucas intimidated and then assigned to assassinate Bloodhound as repayment for lost Kingdom funds.
    Blake Matthews (Mr. Tyrannosaur) Colleague Kingdom enforcer. Lucas socialized with him outside of operations, inviting him to a strip club after a Kingdom meeting, and the two met jointly with Aaron McKinley to collect debts.
    Maria Valdez (Mrs. Heartbeat) Colleague Kingdom assassin and medic who accompanied Lucas during the Halloween warehouse recruitment meeting and the zoo heist operation.

    Trivia¶

    • Lucas received a toy lie detector for Christmas in 1990 at age five, an early spark for his lifelong fascination with truth and deception.
    • He maintains a collection of vintage polygraph machines, fascinated by the history and evolution of mechanical lie detection technology.
    • Lucas is an avid poker player who used his powers to detect bluffs, giving him a significant edge at the table.
    • He enjoys classic detective novels, particularly those featuring Sherlock Holmes and Hercule Poirot, characters renowned for penetrating deception.
    • His fondness for classic jazz music is noted as a relaxation habit during downtime, and the connection between jazz improvisation and authentic expression may parallel his power's relationship to spoken truth.
    • He was the first Kingdom member to physically encounter Sam Small, establishing the adversarial relationship between the Kingdom and the protagonist that would drive much of the story's central conflict.
    • The "Agent Parker" alias he used while impersonating an NSRA agent was paired with badge number 7742.
    • His power extraction by Snake Oil produced a blue-green crystal, which was subsequently processed into Fly, the metahuman drug distributed by Rogue Wave.
    • Lawrence Sullivan (Monkey Business), the leader of Rogue Wave, acquired Lucas's lie-detection power after having Snake Oil extract it, making Sullivan the current wielder of the ability Lucas lost.
    • He and Darnell Hayes (Mr. Nothing) are described as "heterosexual life mates" in the Kevin Smith sense, despite having only met when the Kingdom was founded.
