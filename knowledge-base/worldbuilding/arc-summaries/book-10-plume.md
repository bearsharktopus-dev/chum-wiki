## Summary

Book 10 opens with Sam, still bandaged, giving deposition testimony about Aaron's arrest. She overhears that Aaron's defense is funded by mysterious "concerned citizens for due process" through law firm Tremont & Fairfax - a thread the Auditors will pull for the rest of the arc. At home, Sam and Kate attend physical therapy together and maintain a cold detente in which neither acknowledges the growing evidence that Kate is the chemical-smoke vigilante Soot. Jordan introduces a police scanner and the team investigates pepper-sprayed drug dealers - their first hint that someone else is fighting the Jump trade.

The first major crisis arrives when the Kingdom ambushes the prison transport of Nothing and Mudslide. Interlude RG.1 follows Captain Plasma's POV as the convoy - decoys, anti-ESP shielding, aerial surveillance - is systematically dismantled by Kingdom operatives: T-Rex on the turnpike, Mrs. Fulcrum flipping vehicles, Yellowjacket conducting precision air bursts, and Mrs. Jellyjam suffocating Plasma with a gelatinous substance. BM.2 follows T-Rex and the rescue team to a Kingdom steakhouse in Lancaster, revealing organizational dynamics - Yellowjacket runs the Baltimore branch, Jellyjam shows leadership instincts, the Kingdom's money-laundering infrastructure is laid bare. Sam monitors the transport collapse via scanner, then publicly confronts Richardson at a school assembly about Duvall's death and Kingdom connections, going viral on NetSphere.

The political landscape shifts when Councilman Ward and Richardson introduce the Superhuman Activity Regulation Act (SARA), banning vigilante activities for minors. It passes 12-5. The Young Defenders are dissolved - Gossamer leaves, Connor departs for civilian life, Rampart joins the DVDs, while Sam and Blink refuse to quit. The team reorganizes as "The Auditors" with Amelia and Lily formally joining. Interlude So.1 provides Soot's POV for the first time: a calculated vigilante who infiltrates dealer gatherings, processes chemicals, and reveals motivation centered on helping their father - before Bloodhound tracks them down and they surrender Jump pills. Sam's 16th birthday gives way to a midnight patrol where she and Maggie witness Bash using a mysterious autoinjector (distinct from Jump or Fly) to enhance his powers during a gang fight, before they are arrested for obstruction.

Interlude MR.4 reveals Richardson recruiting Patriot, Turbo Jett, Miasma, and Captain Devil into a government-sanctioned team (Argus Corps), appealing to each one's need for redemption or legitimacy. That same night, Porcelain - a high-ranking operative from the global mercenary group Red Calf - demands Maya help extract someone (implied to be Deathgirl) from Daedalus, and purchases Hypeman for Red Calf. The Auditors infiltrate Rogue Wave's Jump distribution event at a Pennsport marina, where Monkey Business reveals his supernatural contract-binding power - a quarter of the crowd is already under his control. The operation explodes into a multi-sided brawl. Rogue Wave then hijacks NBC10 and the emergency alert system to broadcast their manifesto, claiming 30,000 contracted individuals and declaring war on the Kingdom. Jordan's reckless test at school - deliberately claiming Kingdom allegiance to provoke sleeper agents - exposes Officer Ridley as Rogue Wave-contracted, getting both Sam and Jordan suspended.

The climax unfolds across four chapters. From house arrest, the Auditors trace Tremont & Fairfax's corporate filings to Stheno Pharmaceuticals, a suspicious biomedical front registered near a known Kingdom location. The team infiltrates the Stheno warehouse on Trenton Avenue. The operation goes loud when Soot appears fighting Kingdom enforcers Bash and Lenny (forcefield generator). Argus Corps arrives for an off-the-books operation - Captain Devil tracks through smoke, Turbo Jett attacks with super-speed and heat, Miasma floods the space with toxic gas. Soot announces intent to release chlorine gas for a chemical chain reaction. Sam prioritizes rescuing a zip-tied security guard upstairs; Patriot pursues her and crushes her ankle before Jordan severs the stairs. Sam cuts the guard free, breaks the window, and jumps - landing on a cloak held by Jordan and Blink. The warehouse explodes behind them. The Auditors escape via taxi, laughing hysterically, as Sam glimpses Soot on a nearby rooftop. Act One of the story ends with emergency services rushing toward the explosion.

## Themes

- **The three-body problem** - Philadelphia's conflict expands from a two-sided struggle into a chaotic three-way war between the Kingdom of Keys, Rogue Wave, and the state (via Argus Corps). The Auditors are caught between all three forces, hunted by criminals and government alike, unable to ally fully with any faction. The warehouse climax literalizes this: Kingdom enforcers, Argus Corps, and Soot all fight simultaneously while the Auditors try to escape with evidence.

- **Contracts, control, and consent** - Monkey Business's binding contracts serve as the arc's central metaphor: people surrender autonomy in exchange for something they want (drugs, money, power). This mirrors Richardson's legislation (surrendering freedom for safety), the Kingdom's hierarchy (loyalty enforced through threat), and even Sam's implicit contract with heroism (sacrificing normalcy for responsibility). Everyone in Book 10 is bound by something they didn't fully understand when they agreed to it.

- **The outsider's burden** - Official institutions consistently fail: the prison transport is ambushed, SARA bans the teenagers who are actually fighting crime, Argus Corps is staffed with morally compromised adults on an off-the-books leash. The Auditors - a group of high schoolers operating from a music hall - produce better intelligence than the DVDs and more meaningful disruption than the police, yet they're the ones getting arrested and suspended.

- **Investigation as weapon** - Book 10 marks the Auditors' evolution from street-level vigilantes to intelligence operatives. Their most significant victories come not from fights but from following paper trails: Tremont & Fairfax's statistical anomalies, Stheno Pharmaceuticals' corporate filings, the USB drives stolen from the warehouse. The pen proves mightier than the fist - though the arc ends with an explosion, the real prize is data.

- **End of innocence / End of Act One** - The arc closes Year Two and what the author calls "Act One (of Three)." The landscape has transformed completely from Book 1: organized crime operates at a national and international scale (Red Calf), the government fields its own cape team, the drug crisis has spawned supernatural mind-control networks, and Sam's teenage team is wanted by every faction. The warehouse explosion serves as both a literal and figurative detonation of the status quo - everything that comes next will be different.

## Threads Planted

- **Monkey Business's contract-binding power** - Rogue Wave's leader possesses a supernatural ability to create binding contracts with physiological and psychological compulsion; the mechanism, limits, and origin of this power are unexplored; contracts appear unbreakable (Chs. 156–158)
- **Rogue Wave's organizational structure and declaration of war** - Rogue Wave reveals its full leadership (Monkey Business, Birthday Suit, Rush Order, Dr. Snake Oil, Dead Drop, Jackpot), claims 30,000+ contracted individuals in Philadelphia and 60,000 along the eastern seaboard (Jordan identifies both as likely inflated), hijacks NBC10 to broadcast a manifesto declaring war on the Kingdom (Ch. 158)
- **Soot as independent vigilante** - A chemical-smoke-powered vigilante emerges who absorbs and releases gases, demonstrates martial arts proficiency, processes chemicals in abandoned warehouses, and claims motivation of helping their father; mounting evidence (build, schedule, regional focus, chemical knowledge, Kate's 3 AM returns, access to Sam's team chat) suggests Soot is Kate Smith, but neither confirms it openly (Chs. 150, 153, So.1, 159–160)
- **Argus Corps** - Richardson's government-sanctioned superhero team (Patriot, Turbo Jett, Miasma, Captain Devil) operates under NSRA Special Directive 14-B with detention powers and mandatory body cameras, but its first field appearance is an off-the-books warehouse operation; the team's true scope exceeds public announcement (Chs. 160, MR.5, 163–164)
- **Porcelain and Red Calf** - A global mercenary organization enters the story through Porcelain, who demands Maya help extract someone (heavily implied to be Deathgirl) from Daedalus Correctional Facility and purchases Hypeman for Red Calf's use; escalates the conflict to international scale (MR.4)
- **Stheno Pharmaceuticals and the Auditors' USB evidence** - Kingdom front company registered by T&F junior partner Martin Calloway; warehouse destroyed by Soot's chemical chain reaction, but Jordan stole USB drives containing unknown evidence about Kingdom drug operations; the contents and their implications are unexplored (Chs. 161–162, 164)
- **Tremont & Fairfax connections** - Major law firm handles a statistically outsized number of superhuman defense cases; connected to Aaron McKinley's funded defense, Stheno Pharmaceuticals' incorporation, and Argus Corps' legal paperwork via Katherine Huang; the full scope of the firm's role in Philadelphia's superhuman ecosystem is unexplored (Chs. 149, 151, 161)
- **Aaron McKinley's mysterious defense funding** - "Concerned citizens for due process" financing Aaron's expensive defense through Tremont & Fairfax; source and motivation unknown (Ch. 149)
- **Bash's autoinjector enhancement** - Kingdom enforcer uses a mysterious autoinjector substance (distinct from Jump or Fly) to dramatically enhance his existing powers, then appears at the Stheno warehouse showing similar enhancement; suggests Hypeman is being deployed at the street level (Chs. 154, 162–163)
- **Captain Devil's abilities** - Argus Corps member can track through Soot's smoke and creates an instinctual fear response in Sam; the nature and full extent of his powers are unexplored (Chs. 163–164)
- **Miasma's selective behavior** - During the warehouse battle, Miasma intentionally avoids confronting Sam, suggesting a hidden agenda or allegiance beyond Argus Corps' stated mission (Ch. 164)
- **Kate's anonymous benefactors** - Kate Smith's father's debts are mysteriously paid off by anonymous donors, enabling their planned move; the source is unknown and Sam is skeptical (Ch. 154)
- **Officer Ridley and institutional infiltration** - School security officer exposed as a Rogue Wave sleeper agent through Jordan's deliberate provocation; demonstrates how deeply contracted individuals have penetrated civilian institutions (Ch. 159)

## Threads Progressed

- **Maya Richardson's political rise** (planted Book 7, MR.1) - Richardson introduces the Superhuman Activity Regulation Act, successfully banning minor vigilantism (12-5 vote); forms Argus Corps as a government-sanctioned superhero team; meets with Red Calf's Porcelain, revealing her entanglement extends to international criminal interests; her approval rating takes a hit after Sam's public confrontation but she consolidates institutional power (Chs. 151–152, 160, MR.4, MR.5)

- **Jump/Fly drug crisis** (planted Book 5, Ch. 73) - Jump distribution continues expanding with Rogue Wave using it as a recruitment tool; dealers sign binding contracts for distribution rights; the Kensington streets show worsening conditions; community defense groups ("neighbors with baseball bats") emerge as civilian response (Chs. 150, 154–157)

- **Sam's identity management** (planted Book 1) - Sam gives deposition in costume while bandaged (legally risky); publicly confronts Richardson at school assembly, connecting Bloodhound's activities to civilian Sam; goes viral on NetSphere; suspended for Jordan's provocation; school enrollment declining due to incidents linked to her (Chs. 149, 152, 159)
- **Playback and Marionette's operations** (planted Book 6, Ch. 100) - Referenced in context of broader cape ecosystem but not directly featured in this arc

## Threads Resolved

- **Mr. Nothing and Mudslide's imprisonment** - The Kingdom successfully liberates both operatives from the prison transport through an elaborate highway ambush, resolving the question of whether and how the Kingdom would attempt a rescue; Captain Plasma is incapacitated, and the convoy is overwhelmed despite extensive precautions (planted Book 8 Ch. 135, resolved RG.1)
- **Young Defenders dissolution** - The Superhuman Activity Regulation Act passes 12-5, formally banning minor vigilantism; the Young Defenders are dissolved as a team - Gossamer leaves superhero work, Connor departs for civilian life, Rampart joins the DVDs officially - while Sam and Blink refuse to quit and reconstitute as the Auditors (planted Book 6-7, resolved Chs. 151, 153)
- **Stheno Pharmaceuticals warehouse** - The Auditors identify the Kingdom front company through corporate records investigation, infiltrate the warehouse to gather evidence, and the facility is destroyed when Soot triggers a chemical chain reaction with stored chlorine gas; the Auditors escape with USB drives but the production site is eliminated (planted Ch. 161, resolved Ch. 164)
