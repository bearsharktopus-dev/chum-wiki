## Background

Kill Buddha is an unidentified metahuman operative who began appearing in Northeast and South Philadelphia's underground in the spring of 2026. They were first reported secondhand as a "new four-armed vigilante with a baseball bat" circling the city's metahuman fight clubs, and surfaced on-page as a competitor at a South Philadelphia underground fight club, where they fought masked, never spoke, accepted no interviews, and left without being seen.

Behind the fight-club appearances, Kill Buddha conducted a methodical intelligence operation: over at least a month they approached solo operators and small organizations across South Philly, West Philly, and Kensington with the same set of questions - who do you know, who are they connected to, what can they do, where do they operate, who protects them - assembling what amounts to a social-graph map of every powered person in Philadelphia. The purpose of the map, and the party it was being built for, were never disclosed. They moved through neighborhoods far faster than a single person should be able to, and secured meetings with people who do not meet strangers.

By their own framing, Kill Buddha is a professional working "a job" on a deadline, under instructions to avoid unnecessary friction with established local operators.

### Abilities

- **Supernumerary limbs / "four-dimensional" physiology** - Kill Buddha can extend additional limbs - up to four arms and, in extremity, four legs - which emerge through their own flesh in a way that does not correspond to ordinary anatomy. Their torso and limbs can "rotate" or blend through space without a corresponding movement of the hips. To a blood-sense observer, Kill Buddha reads as **two complete, simultaneously-live circulatory systems**, with vessels that branch and then terminate "into nothing" - as though part of the body extends in a direction that cannot be perceived. The effect has been described as a cross-section of a higher-dimensional object rotated into three-dimensional space. The skull reads as the most ordinary part of the body; the limbs the least.
- **Vulnerable skull** - Because the head is the most "normal" part of their anatomy, Kill Buddha avoids head contact, retracting the extra limbs when their skull is threatened.
- **Accelerated self-repair** - Visible bruising sustained in a fight reduces and disappears within seconds.
- **Predictive footwork** - Their movement is not unusually fast; it is *preemptive*. They read an opponent's weight shifts and are in position before a strike begins - prediction rather than reflex.
- **Elite hand-to-hand and grappling** - Trained well beyond any combat sport, with lethal, target-specific technique that they deliberately restrain in the ring (pulling shots that would otherwise hit the throat, ears, or joints). A four-limbed grappling game that defeats two-armed defenses (e.g. the standard sprawl); capable, when pressed, of competent orthodox boxing as well.

### Communication

Kill Buddha does not speak in public, communicating instead through **American Sign Language** and a typed phone screen (with pre-written macros). They are not mute - they are capable of speech in a layered, two-toned "chord" voice (two throats at once) - but withhold it, apparently to deny listeners voice data. They wear a hard (ceramic or wood) **Noh-style mask** with large yellow eyes and red brows.

### Equipment

- A baseball bat, carried but characterized by Kill Buddha as "a prop."
- A phone used for typed communication and for recording field notes.

## Personality

Cold, exact, and patient. Kill Buddha is socially absent - they do not play to crowds, shake hands, or acknowledge opponents, win or lose - and they treat people as nodes in a map. They distinguish their own behavior sharply from mercy: declining to hurt someone they could hurt is, in their framing, "not restraint - patience. There is a difference." Their private register runs to open misanthropy. They are meticulous, professional, and difficult to keep eyes on.

The one consistent exception to their flat affect is a dry, condescending streak of trash talk delivered in sign language during fights ("good try, you almost had me, keep at it, sport" - but mean).

## Arc Appearances

??? warning "Book 17: Rules of Engagement"

    **Role:** Minor (offstage)

    Kill Buddha is first referenced secondhand in Magdalene O'Brien's South Philadelphia field report (Ch. 261) as a "new four-armed vigilante with a baseball bat" sniffing around metahuman underground fight clubs, surfaced through Kate Smith's contacts. They do not appear on-page in this book.

??? warning "Book 18: Fire at Will"

    **Role:** Major (antagonist / arc mystery)

    Kill Buddha appears on-page at Douglas Furr's South Philadelphia underground fight club, where Sam Small (attending as a spectator, then competing under the ring name "Superbeast") watches them dismantle a copper-bodied fighter, Cocoa Carl, by submission while visibly pulling every lethal shot. Over the following weeks they fight several more bouts, all with the same clinical restraint, and Sam's blood sense files their impossible vascular signature as unidentifiable.

    Behind the scenes, Kill Buddha confronts Bloodhound II (Derek Taylor) about the identity of Megalodon, and Derek - pulling on the thread afterward - discovers that Kill Buddha has been running the same interrogation across the city, building a comprehensive social-graph map of Philadelphia's powered population for an undisclosed purpose. Kill Buddha then singles Sam out for a personal exhibition bout, winning a split decision but treating her, afterward, as a peer.

    For the identity behind the mask, and the resolution of Kill Buddha's operation, see the spoiler section below.

??? warning "Identity (Book 18: Fire at Will spoiler)"

    **Kill Buddha is [Fatima al-Qadi](fatima-al-qadi.md)** - the seventeen-year-old "regenerator" who had spent Books 17-18 embedded in the community around the Tacony Community Center and, over the same summer, in a romantic relationship with Sam Small.

    The romance and the fight-club mystery were the same operation. Fatima's assignment was to integrate into the powered community around the center and confirm whether Sam Small was the vigilante Megalodon; Sam, in her own words, was cultivated as "an asset." The four arms, the bizarre blood signature Sam kept failing to place, the ASL/two-throat persona, and the social-graph mapping were all Kill Buddha; the smooth-skinned, elusive girlfriend was the cover.

    The operation was exposed during the finale: Aaron McKinley (Mr. Prometheus) took Fatima hostage as bait for Sam, not knowing what she was. When Aaron decided to kill her, Fatima - who had let herself be captured and had concealed a switchblade and two extra arms the whole time - killed him with a stab to the abdomen, blowing her cover to do it. She confronted Sam, asked her outright whether she was Megalodon (Sam refused to answer), declined Sam's offer of sanctuary, and left through a window, vanishing the way Kill Buddha always did.

    A between-arcs interlude confirms her employer: **Red Calf.** Fatima/Kill Buddha is a Red Calf operative who reports to Porcelain (Chezki Espinosa) alongside Gallows (Samuel Royce); the arc-long operation was Red Calf intelligence-gathering to confirm Megalodon's identity. She ultimately assessed Sam at only "30%" (the simultaneous "Superbeast" and "Megalodon" sightings created doubt), and recommended Red Calf disengage from Philadelphia and pursue psychological warfare against Sam rather than assassination, since killing her "makes a martyr." Still open: whether "Fatima al-Qadi" is a real name. See [Fatima al-Qadi](fatima-al-qadi.md) for the civilian-identity record.

## Trivia

- The name is taken from the Zen koan "If you meet the Buddha on the road, kill him." Douglas Furr's ring introduction renders it "If You See Buddha On The Road, Kill Him."
- Kill Buddha tips the fight-club emcee for their introductions - noted as unusual, since most fighters do not.
- They have repeatedly left a crowded basement venue without anyone - including trained observers - seeing them go.
- Maggie O'Brien, who can read ASL, is the only member of Sam's circle able to translate Kill Buddha's in-ring trash talk.
