## Background

Douglas Furr runs an unsanctioned metahuman fight club out of a dead-storefront basement on the South Philadelphia / lower Center City border - a knock-pattern door, a doorman with a cashbox, and a real twenty-by-twenty ring. He ran a similar circuit in Baltimore before moving up to Philadelphia, and the Philadelphia room is, in the words of the regulars, "his room": he built it, he maintains it, and if it breaks, it is on him.

As emcee he announces the card; as matchmaker he vets and pairs fighters; as proprietor he is the final authority ("if you have a problem with the ref, you have a problem with me, and if you have a problem with me, you have a problem with the door"). New fighters talk to him first, are watched across a few visits, and are matched by overall threat level rather than by specific ability - the logic that lets him put a copper-bodied brawler against a four-armed grappler but never against someone who can melt metal. He keeps liability waivers on hand and a medic (Dr. Demonic) on site; the room has standards even without a license.

## Powers & Abilities

### PERKS Assessment

No PERKS assessment on file. It is unclear whether Douglas Furr is metahuman; his matchmaking "sense" may be a power or simply the product of long experience.

### Abilities

- **Threat assessment / matchmaking instinct** - An unusually accurate read on a fighter's overall danger and on which matchups are safe. Sam (Superbeast) cannot tell whether this is a metahuman ability or hard-won expertise.
- **Crowd command** - Runs a room "not because he's loud but because he's calibrated"; his announcing voice fills the basement "like it was designed to fit the space."

### Training & Skills

- **People-reading** - Catalogues newcomers at a glance; immediately clocked Sam as "interesting" and seemed to suspect she was already powered before she admitted it.
- **Operational discipline** - Vets fighters over multiple visits, enforces no-recording and phones-away rules, keeps waivers and a medic, and matches conservatively to keep the room from "breaking."

## Personality

Tall, thin, and unhurried - he "moves through crowds by existing at a slightly different altitude than everyone else." He is talkative in the manner of someone who made talking his profession, but underneath it is constant assessment: he scans a room on entry, files people into categories he does not announce, and remembers the faces he finds interesting. He is careful rather than worried ("I'm not worried. I'm paying attention. There's a difference"), and he treats the safety of his room as a personal responsibility. With prospects he is a patient recruiter, leaving doors open and letting fighters feel that walking through was their own idea.

## Trivia

- "Douglas Furr" is a pun on the Douglas fir tree, fitting the punny-codename pattern of the fight club's roster (Cocoa Carl, Mr. Stupendous, etc.).
- He folds his deli-bag lunch into a neat square every time he eats at the venue - a small tell Sam reads as a sign of the kind of person he is.
- He keeps assumption-of-risk waivers physically on hand, written to about eighty-five percent legal accuracy and one hundred percent seriousness; one clause covers "other consequences not foreseeable at time of signing" (e.g., the time two fighters' powers turned each other periwinkle for a week).
- He suggested "Living Dead Girl" would have been a better Rob Zombie reference than "Superbeast."
