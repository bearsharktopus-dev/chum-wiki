## Background

Samuel Royce served as a U.S. Marine Corps sniper before leaving the Corps under circumstances he does not discuss. By 2006-2007 he had transitioned into the private consulting world and was part of the international specialist roster brought to the Daedalus Correctional Facility construction site, where he met Chezki Espinosa, Jared Cross, Anika Voss, and Retired General Hartley. Espinosa later recognized this group as "tools that have realized they're being used" - government operators who saw through the institutions they served.

When Espinosa broke with Israel circa 2014-2015 and Cross invited him to Zurich, the Daedalus-era contacts became the founding roster of Red Calf Security Solutions in 2015. Royce has served as the organization's precision marksman and overwatch specialist since.

In the spring of 2026, Royce deployed to Philadelphia as part of the Red Calf cell activated to degrade the Joint FBI-NSRA Daedalus Task Force. Operating under the callsign "Golf" within the NATO-phonetic cell rotation (Antfarm, Barefoot, Cypress, Dogwalker, Empty, Famine, Gallows, Higgs), he is one of approximately three powered members of the eight-operator Philadelphia cell - and, per Crossroads's post-warehouse assessment, the most dangerous of them.

### Abilities

- **Surface Adhesion / Wall Climbing** - Royce's body can adhere to and brace against vertical and inverted surfaces. Demonstrated uses during the Tacony warehouse fight include lying flat on the underside of a highway-facing billboard to sniper-brace a rifle against the structural crossbeams (Ch. 258); scaling a vertical brick wall by flipping his body from back-to-the-wall to feet-on-the-wall in a single motion "with a speed that blood sense tracks but the brain can't process"; and planting a single foot to a catwalk grating with enough adhesion that an attempt to pull him over by the ankle moved him zero distance. The ability allows him to fight from angles that defeat conventional perimeter assumptions. It does not appear to affect his blood-sense visibility; Miss Megalodon was able to track his circulation throughout the encounter.
- **Precision Marksmanship** - Royce is a superhumanly trained sniper even before his power is factored in. Miss Megalodon observes that a standing target in open asphalt, shot at from a billboard underside, was being hit with surgical precision that accounted for the shallow downward angle.

### Limitations

- **Blood-Sense Visibility** - His adhesion power does not conceal him from Miss Megalodon's blood sense, which can track him behind walls and above ceilings at close-to-medium range.
- **Physiological Vulnerability** - He is not durable at the Porcelain level and can be injured by close-quarters combat. Miss Megalodon cracked ribs, struck his sternum, split his jaw, and raked puncture wounds into the backside of his calf and the inside of his knee during their Ch. 258 engagement. Post-raid DVD assessment at the start of the tracking operation: "Royce is degraded from injuries sustained during the warehouse engagement."
- **Requires Line-Of-Sight For Optical Engagement** - Like any sniper, his engagement profile depends on sightlines; an opponent with penetrating sensory capabilities and the willingness to close into CQB can neutralize the range advantage.

### Training & Skills

- **Sniping** - U.S. Marine Corps training plus years of PMC field experience. Standard operational caliber: .308 Winchester per DVD assessment. None of the other Red Calf Philadelphia operators use anything larger.
- **Close-Quarters Combat** - Knife work (fixed-blade), unarmed takedowns, garrote (chain), tear-gas deployment, pistol transitions. During the Ch. 258 mezzanine fight he probed the Megalodon suit for soft gaps between armor panels with a methodical thrust pattern targeting tendons and veins.
- **Rearguard and Ambush Tactics** - Trained to cover evacuation routes and catch pursuing breach teams in crossfire from elevated or unexpected positions.
- **Interrogation Under Combat Conditions** - Demonstrated mid-fight interrogation technique ("You one of the DVD kids? I was briefed on the DVD kids. You're new") aimed at extracting attribution information while engaging.
- **Professional Discipline** - When tear gas contamination began cutting into his own operational capacity, he rationally chose extraction over completing a kill, stomping his target's ankle as a compromise between neutralization and escape.

## Personality

Royce communicates in flat, unimpressed, East Coast-accented nasal tones. He is professional, deliberate, and patient under pressure. He did not raise his voice when he recognized that an unknown armored combatant had closed to knife range; he adjusted. He does not appear to take pleasure in killing or in combat banter, though he will carry on casual intelligence-gathering conversations mid-fight ("Freelance gets people killed, kid.").

He maintains Red Calf's professional register: no costumes, no demands, no negotiations. Objectives are executed, obstacles are removed, and withdrawal happens cleanly. When opponents fail to behave the way targets are supposed to behave - standing up after a leg shot, tracking him blind through tear gas - his observable adjustment is recalibration, not emotion.

Espinosa's characterization of the former-government operators he recruited to Red Calf applies directly to Royce: a man who recognized the systems he served were using him, left without explaining why, and now offers his specialist capabilities to a buyer who does not pretend otherwise. His silence about why he left the Marines is treated, within Red Calf, as dignity rather than secrecy.

## Trivia

- Red Calf Philadelphia cell callsigns follow the NATO phonetic alphabet: Antfarm, Barefoot, Cypress, Dogwalker, Empty, Famine, Gallows, Higgs. Royce's position, "Golf," corresponds to his codename's initial letter.
- His standard rifle caliber per DVD intelligence is .308 Winchester. None of the other Red Calf Philadelphia operators use anything larger, which shaped Sam's body armor requirements for the tracking operation (level IV ceramic plates).
- The "thunderclap rather than crack" report of his sniper fire is audible across several blocks, per Jasmine Walker's uncle's description of the ordnance he heard during the Tacony warehouse raid.
- The adhesion ability does not hide him from Miss Megalodon's blood sense, which tracked him continuously throughout the Ch. 258 engagement including during the tear-gas blackout.
- Rampart specifically wanted him "behind bars" in the post-raid message to Sam; in its absence, the ongoing federal concern is that Red Calf could position Gallows on a water tower at a future date and end Miss Megalodon's civilian identity with a single shot.
- His appearance in combat (Ch. 258): blonde hair, brown sideburns, a bandana wrapped around the lower half of his face with a skull-jaw design on it. The skull bandana is a personal marker rather than a Red Calf uniform element.
