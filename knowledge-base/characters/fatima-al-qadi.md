## Background

Fatima first came to the Tacony Community Center after her cousin was treated at the scene of a multi-vehicle accident on Roosevelt Boulevard (the May 16, 2026 pileup at Roosevelt and Rhawn). The EMTs who stabilized her cousin mentioned that one of them worked at the community center in Tacony, and Fatima dropped in unannounced one Saturday to thank her in person. Her cousin had sustained a broken collarbone and bruising and was recovering at home. That visit introduced her to Sam Small, who gave her a tour of the building.

Her living situation is "complicated." She has described her family as "not big on creature comforts" and says she does not own a cellular phone, relying on an MP3 player for music. She has not disclosed her address, her school, her guardians, or her work, and has not been asked. Sam extended a standing invitation to return to the center without appointment, which Fatima has accepted, coming by multiple afternoons a week since her first visit.

She is Muslim.

## Personality

Fatima is warm, direct, and observant. She smiles easily, listens attentively (not performatively), and tolerates silence without discomfort. She does not push on information she is not given, and she does not volunteer information she is not asked for. She shares lightly - about music, about small pleasures, about what she's seen - and returns conversation to common ground when it drifts toward the personal.

She processes music tactilely rather than lyrically - she can sing along note-for-note to songs whose lyrics she has never examined, and when Sam pointed out that the Morrissey song she was humming was violently hostile toward its subject, her response was mild surprise and the observation that "the music is the point for me." This processing style extends to how she relates to objects and people in general: she registers textures and presence first and verbal content second.

Her stated philosophy, articulated to Sam on a cot in the community center's third-floor shelter space: "You should get a thing. Everyone should have a thing that's just theirs. Something that's not useful, not productive. Just... yours." Her own stated "thing" is her music collection - an 80s/90s industrial, goth, and post-punk canon far older than she is (Depeche Mode, Sisters of Mercy, Bauhaus, The Smiths, Ministry, KMFDM, Skinny Puppy) - that she has listened to, apparently, for most of her conscious life.

## Arc Appearances

??? warning "Book 17: Rules of Engagement"

    **Role:** Major (Sam's love interest)

    Fatima first appears in Chapter 260 as a walk-in at the Tacony Community Center, explaining that her cousin had been in the Roosevelt Boulevard pileup the previous week and that the EMTs had mentioned the center. Sam gives her a tour. She discloses regeneration as her power but declines to discuss classification, and she asks whether she can return without making an appointment. Sam tells her no appointment is needed.

    She returns in Chapter 261, first verbally dismantling a trio of Songbirds outside the front entrance and then joining Sam in the center's third-floor emergency shelter space. The two of them share a single earbud through Fatima's MP3 player - Depeche Mode, KMFDM, Bauhaus, The Smiths, Sisters of Mercy - and Fatima articulates her philosophy that "everyone should have a thing that's just theirs." Sam, for one of the first times on-page, experiences genuine unclenched quiet. Fatima ends the visit leaning on Sam's shoulder.

    Sam invites her to junior prom on short notice mid-week; Fatima accepts instantly. Chapter 263 covers prom night in detail. Fatima arrives at the Small house in a dark green dress with both wrists' worth of thin gold bangles and meets Ben and Rachel; the meeting is warm and smooth. At the Celebration Hall on Harbison Avenue she reveals a bifid tongue piercing she performed herself, slow-dances with Sam across multiple songs, encourages Sam to "let yourself" be present, and accepts Sam's mother's offer of a sleepover afterward. The night ends with Sam and Fatima together in Sam's bedroom in Mayfair. Sam remains awake into the early hours of May 31 with Fatima asleep beside her, unable fully to put down the shape of the Red Calf operation that had concluded the same night.

??? warning "Book 18: Fire at Will"

    **Role:** Major (Sam's girlfriend)

    Over the early summer, Fatima becomes a fixture of Sam's life. After weeks of undefined closeness - shared earbuds on the third-floor cots, Wawa runs, Fatima drawn into Small family dinners - Sam asks her directly what they are; Fatima answers by kissing her, and they are officially together (Ch. 267). It is in this conversation that Fatima discloses her history: a war-orphaned refugee, passed between increasingly distant relatives, who underwent an early hysterectomy and whose mild regeneration "locked in" an incomplete puberty. She meets Sam's ex, Jamila Fayad, without incident (though Jamila privately reads her as "very charming" in a way that is not quite a compliment). She attends Rachel Small's July 18 accessibility fundraiser as Sam's date and is the one Sam sends to evacuate Rachel when Mr. Prometheus attacks.

    Fatima al-Qadi is the four-armed operative [Kill Buddha](kill-buddha.md). The relationship and the summer-long fight-club mystery were a single intelligence operation: Fatima's assignment was to embed in the powered community around the Tacony Community Center and confirm whether Sam Small was the vigilante Megalodon - Sam was, in her own words, cultivated as "an asset." Her elusive backstory, her smooth scar-resistant skin, the bizarre blood signature Sam kept failing to place, and her habit of being "whatever she needs to be" were all of a piece with the masked operative.

    The cover broke at the finale. Aaron McKinley (Mr. Prometheus) abducted Fatima as bait to lure Sam to a Kensington trap house, not knowing what she was. Fatima had allowed the capture - she had concealed a switchblade and two extra arms the entire time, intending to extract herself cleanly - and when Aaron moved to kill her, she killed him instead, stabbing him in the abdomen and blowing her cover to do it. In the burning house she confronted Sam, revealed the job, and asked her outright whether she was Megalodon. Sam refused to answer, told Fatima she loved her regardless, and offered her open-ended sanctuary at the center (the third-floor cots, window left unlocked). Fatima declined, warned Sam that "your sentimentality is going to get you killed one day," and left through the window. Sam - resisting the compulsion to chase her - let her go.

    A between-arcs interlude confirms her employer: **Red Calf.** Fatima reports to Porcelain (Chezki Espinosa) alongside Gallows (Samuel Royce); her arc-long mission was a Red Calf intelligence operation to confirm whether Sam was Megalodon. She failed to confirm it (assessing Sam at only "30%" - the simultaneous "Superbeast" and "Megalodon" sightings created doubt) and recommended that Red Calf disengage from Philadelphia and pressure Sam out of public life through psychological warfare rather than kill her ("killing Samantha Small will make her a martyr"). Still unclear whether "Fatima al-Qadi" is her real name. The cots-room window at the community center remains unlocked. See [Kill Buddha](kill-buddha.md) for the operative-identity record.
