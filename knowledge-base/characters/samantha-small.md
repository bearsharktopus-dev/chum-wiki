## Background

Samantha Small was a fourteen-year-old Jewish girl living in Mayfair, Philadelphia when a boating accident on Pop-Pop Moe's boat in August 2023 triggered her Activation Event, granting shark-derived metahuman abilities including enhanced bite force, blood-sensing, accelerated regeneration, and tooth generation from any part of her body. Registered through the JLUMA system shortly after, she was recruited into the Young Defenders by Liberty Belle (Diane Williams), who became her first mentor. Sam's early career as the vigilante Bloodhound was a brutal education: within her first year, she witnessed a Kingdom of Keys execution, had her family home destroyed, was tortured by Aaron McKinley, watched Liberty Belle die in her arms, and sustained massive radiation exposure confronting Chernobyl alone. During her subsequent hospitalization, she was diagnosed with PTSD and bipolar disorder alongside her pre-existing ADHD, began treatment with lithium, and met her maternal grandmother Camilla de Leon for the first time.

After recovering, Sam co-founded the Auditors with Jordan Westwood, Connor Spinelli, and Joshua Pleasants (Miasma) - an independent team operating out of the Tacony Music Hall, dedicated to exposing the Kingdom of Keys. Over the next two years, she evolved from impulsive field operative to strategic leader, guiding the Auditors through escalating conflicts involving the Kingdom, the power-drug network Rogue Wave, the government-sanctioned Argus Corps, and individual antagonists including the returning Aaron McKinley and the white supremacist Shrike. She navigated anti-vigilante legislation pushed by Councilwoman Maya Richardson, negotiated a protection arrangement with Rogue Wave to safeguard her family, and began mentoring powered youth through a community center program established by Councilman Jamal Davis.

Sam retired the Bloodhound identity after killing Niles Nolan (Shrike) in a dissociative episode during their final confrontation, passing the mantle to Derek Taylor as Bloodhound II. After months of attempted retirement spent working as an EMT intern and running the mentorship program, she acknowledged that she had never truly stopped doing vigilante work and adopted the new identity of Miss Megalodon in February 2026. She currently operates in Mayfair under this alias while continuing her roles as peer mentor, EMT trainee, and de facto intelligence coordinator for Philadelphia's independent hero community.

Sam is the daughter of Benjamin Small, a city planner, and Rachel Small (nee Blanc), a librarian. Her paternal grandfather Morris "Pop-pop Moe" Small is a retired engineer with historical connections to Daedalus Correctional Facility and Liberty Belle. Her maternal grandfather Victor Blanc is an estranged figure whose personality reveals troubling parallels with Sam's own tendencies. She has no siblings, stands five feet ten inches tall, and has a broad-shouldered, wide-hipped, rectangular "powerlifter" build.

Her power ranking is modest compared to heavy hitters like Captain Plasma, but her threat level is elevated by tactical intelligence, pain tolerance, and willingness to sustain injuries that would incapacitate other combatants.

### Abilities

- **Enhanced Bite Force** - Bite force dramatically exceeding human norms. She can bite through flesh, bone, and some industrial materials. Her most reliable close-quarters offensive capability.
- **Blood Sense** - Sensory ability to detect and identify blood within a significant radius. She can sense blood on objects, detect internal bleeding, identify drugs or foreign substances in a person's bloodstream, and assess injuries with diagnostic precision. She identifies Jump and Fly users by their fluorescent orange, "fizzy" blood signature from blocks away, and uses long-dried blood particles on Philadelphia's streets as a LIDAR-like map for spatial awareness and navigation. Functions as her primary investigative, medical, and environmental awareness tool.
- **Accelerated Regeneration** - Heals from injuries at a dramatically accelerated rate. She has recovered from crushed tracheas, severe puncture wounds, extensive burns, broken bones, gunshot wounds, and cardiac arrest. Regeneration requires high-calorie, high-protein nutritional support and is not instantaneous; severe injuries still require days to weeks of recovery. Central to her heroic philosophy: she views absorbing damage others cannot survive as a moral obligation.
- **Tooth Generation (Odontogenesis)** - Can grow teeth from any surface of her body. First manifested under extreme stress during Arc 4. Tactical applications include:
  - **Instant Armor** - Growing overlapping teeth across body parts as improvised armor. Complete full-body coverage first manifested during the Shrike confrontation (Arc 14), though it triggers claustrophobia linked to her Activation Event.
  - **Blading** - Growing a single tooth through her forearm as a concealed cutting implement, useful for misdirection.
  - **Tooth-Knuckles** - Her most common offensive application. Teeth from the knuckles enhance striking damage. She developed fine control over tooth sharpness by Arc 5, with gloves slotted for teeth to fit through as pointed knuckledusters.
  - **Shin-Teeth** - A tooth grown through an exposed costume gap for kicks, capable of concentrating enough point pressure to crack Pumice's stone body (Arc 6).
  - **Palm-Teeth** - Teeth from palms for extra grappling grip or as slashing weapons.
  - **Biting Surfaces** - Teeth on forearms and other contact points during grappling.
- **Enhanced Durability** - Physical toughness exceeding baseline humans. Her jaw absorbs punches with minimal effect via a reflexive lockjaw response. She has sustained extraordinary cumulative damage without permanent disability, though Dr. Song has warned of long-term consequences.
- **Equilibrium State** - Discovered during Arc 15 that she can sustain physical activity indefinitely by reaching equilibrium between exertion and regeneration, eliminating normal fatigue.

### Limitations

- Regeneration is not instantaneous; severe injuries require days to weeks of recovery with medical intervention. Dr. Song has noted small benign tumors, chronic organ damage, and extensive scar tissue, warning that regeneration might accelerate cancer.
- Full-body Instant Armor triggers claustrophobia and dissociative episodes rooted in her Activation Event (drowning).
- Tooth generation is physically strenuous and draining.
- Experienced Hypeman withdrawal (headaches, tremors, shaking) after a single use in Arc 11. Nicotine partially alleviates symptoms, leading to an ongoing struggle with cigarettes.
- Blood sense can be overwhelming in environments with large amounts of blood or many injured individuals.
- Baseline-weight human who can be physically overpowered by opponents with superior strength, size, or weaponry.
- Takes lithium, Concerta (methylphenidate), Zofran (as needed), Prazosin, and Desvenlafaxine for bipolar disorder, ADHD, and PTSD. Medication disruption affects cognitive function and emotional regulation.

## Personality

Sam is energetic, self-deprecating, and impulsive, with a moral compass that bends but does not break under extraordinary pressure. Her narration features stream-of-consciousness thoughts, parenthetical asides, and a distinctive blend of teenage vernacular with unexpectedly sharp observations. She possesses an overwhelming "truth drive" - a compulsive need to uncover and expose concealed information, articulated through the phrase "if it can be destroyed by the truth it deserves to be destroyed by the truth," though she lacks the political sophistication to fully appreciate the principle's complexity. She is deeply funny in a self-lacerating way, frequently undercutting heroism or vulnerability with dark humor. In combat, she uses humor as targeted bullying designed to make opponents angry and sloppy.

Beneath the wisecracking exterior, Sam carries extraordinary trauma. Her relationship with danger is paradoxical: not suicidal, but an adrenaline junkie whose overwhelming lust for life manifests as seemingly self-destructive behavior. Her regeneration enables and reinforces this pattern - because she heals from injuries that would permanently disable others, she treats her body as expendable in service of protecting those who cannot recover as she can. Her core philosophical position is that regeneration creates an ethical obligation to absorb harm and to offer mercy repeatedly, even when it fails, because most people only get to make the mistake of showing mercy to the wrong person once.

Sam's mental health is a persistent thread. Her bipolar disorder manifests in identifiable patterns she has learned to self-monitor, including flagging potential hypomanic episodes. She takes medication consistently and has voluntarily requested therapy on multiple occasions. She experiences dissociative episodes, hypervigilance, stress-related hair-pulling, and difficulty sleeping. Her ADHD contributes to her stream-of-consciousness processing style, and her autism manifests in intense focus, pattern recognition, and occasional difficulty with social conventions.

Her relationships are defined by fierce protectiveness and an inability to delegate risk, rooted in a generational self-reliance philosophy traced from Holocaust survivor Leah Small through Pop-pop Moe and Benjamin to Sam herself. Family therapy identified that Sam's vigilantism and her father's gun ownership stem from the same conviction that institutions cannot be trusted to protect loved ones. Multiple people have confronted her self-sacrificial tendencies - Maggie, Jennifer Patterson, and Maxwell Martinez chief among them. The central arc of Sam's character development is her gradual acceptance that she cannot personally be present for every crisis, and that systems she builds can function without her. Maxwell's diagnosis of a "load-bearing life" - where stepping back causes everything to collapse - prompted her to seek sustainability rather than oscillating between burnout and compulsion.
