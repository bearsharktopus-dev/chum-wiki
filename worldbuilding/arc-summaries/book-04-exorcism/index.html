    Book 4: Exorcism¶

    A girl with shark teeth inherits a dead woman's war and learns that the ghost you exorcise may be the one possessing you.

    Summary¶

    In the aftermath of Liberty Belle's death at Chernobyl's hands, Sam attends the reading of her mentor's will and discovers that Belle has bequeathed her entire collection of detective equipment, investigative files, and case notes including a notebook on Illya Myronovych Fedorov. A personal letter explains that Belle chose Sam as her investigative successor because Sam questioned her like a true detective rather than worshipping her as a legend. At Belle's funeral at Laurel Hill East Cemetery, Sam is shaken by the appearance of Mrs. Z, a Kingdom of Keys operative, among the mourners. Over winter break, Sam studies the notebook and discovers Belle's final cryptic note the word "Porcelain?" underlined in red. When NSRA agents arrive at Lily Chen's house to confiscate the materials, Miasma intervenes, exposing their false claims. Sam plays a recording from Belle's final battle in which Chernobyl admits to a government arrangement, and she confronts Councilman Davis, who proposes forming an off-the-books covert team to investigate the NSRA's protection of Chernobyl.

    Sam's investigation is brutally interrupted when Aaron McKinley given a kill order by Mr. Polygraph after his earlier humiliation ambushes her with a crowbar after a routine day of power testing with Dr. Leonard Harris. Joined by Pumice, Daisy/Deathgirl, and Chrysalis, the former Philly Phreaks capture and torture Sam: Aaron removes her fingernails with a claw hammer, and Daisy breaks her left hand. Through psychological manipulation and sheer will, Sam provokes Daisy into a chaotic transformation, discovers she can grow teeth from her wrists and knuckles, saws through her restraints, and escapes after puncturing Aaron's throat and face. She staggers into a residential neighborhood where strangers named Marge and Bill call an ambulance. At a New Year's Eve party at DVD headquarters, Sam reveals her ordeal and her new power to her teammates, struggling to balance her trauma with the celebration and her strained relationship with Jamila.

    The newly formed Auditors Sam, Jordan, and Spindle, with Miasma as a volatile adult advisor establish their headquarters at the Tacony Music Hall. They analyze shell companies linking the NSRA to the Kingdom, conduct surveillance on a suspected covert facility, and discover a listening device in their base. Jordan's abusive mother tracks them down twice: the first time Sam punches her to protect Jordan, the second time Mrs. Westwood calls the police and NSRA agents Torres and Jennings, resulting in Jordan permanently severing ties with their mother. When Miasma is framed for killing four NSRA security guards at the Hatboro-Horsham office, the Auditors investigate the crime scene posing as student journalists and encounter Mr. Polygraph and Mr. ESP impersonating NSRA agents, confirming Kingdom involvement in the frame-up. Agent Torres later visits the Auditors off-record, sharing files on Chernobyl's location in the abandoned Arch Street Subway tunnels and warning them to stay away.

    The Auditors defy Torres's orders and enter the tunnels, where they are ambushed by Mr. ESP (whose psychic power that day is "remote listening," allowing him to have overheard their conversation with Torres) and Mr. Mudslide. In a desperate battle in total darkness, Sam uses her blood as a sensory mapping tool while Jordan and Spindle fight from the shadows. Chernobyl arrives and violently rejects the Kingdom's recruitment offer, hurling Mr. ESP into a concrete pillar. Sam sends Jordan and Spindle to safety and confronts Chernobyl alone despite two gunshot wounds to her abdomen. Their battle is as much philosophical debate as physical combat: Chernobyl invokes the Maccabees and self-defense, Sam counters with her grandfather's Torah teachings and the obligation not to stand idly by. She systematically dismantles his armor, exposing herself to lethal radiation, and when Chernobyl emerges from his suit, she embraces him rather than killing him, asking him to pretend she is his daughter Yulia. Chernobyl shoves her away, weeping, and retreats into his damaged suit. Sam collapses from radiation poisoning. She is admitted to Thomas Jefferson University Hospital as a "Jane Doe" with radiation exposure exceeding ten Grays a normally lethal dose and spends two weeks in a coma while her regeneration fights to save her. On February 23rd, she awakens to learn that Chernobyl has turned himself in to authorities. Her grandfather confirms: she got to him.

    Themes¶

    • Exorcism and inherited haunting The arc's title answers Book 3's "Dybbuk." Chernobyl accused Belle of being possessed by Franklin's ghost; now Sam must determine whether she is possessed by Belle's ghost or acting from her own convictions. Her choice to embrace Chernobyl rather than kill him is the exorcism a deliberate break from the cycle of inherited vengeance.
    • Compassion as weapon and weakness Sam's defining moment is not defeating Chernobyl through violence but through vulnerability: she asks him to pretend she is his daughter. This radical compassion achieves what Belle's brute force could not Chernobyl turns himself in. Yet it nearly kills Sam in the process, interrogating whether mercy and survival can coexist.
    • Institutional betrayal and justified disobedience The NSRA protects Chernobyl, frames Miasma, attempts to confiscate evidence, and sends agents to intimidate a fourteen-year-old. The Kingdom impersonates federal agents. Sam's investigation reveals that the line between government agency and criminal organization is thinner than anyone acknowledged, justifying the Auditors' extralegal existence.
    • The body as battleground Sam's evolving shark biology is both her greatest asset and her most intimate horror. Growing teeth from her knuckles saves her life during torture, but the uncontrollable tooth growth during her hospital stay and the skin she sheds by the pound make her body an alien landscape she cannot fully control.
    • Found family versus blood family Jordan's story crystallizes the arc's family theme: their biological mother is abusive and neglectful, but the Auditors provide genuine love and acceptance. Sam's biological family remains loving but increasingly distant from her reality. The Auditors, formed from necessity, become a family forged in shared danger and mutual sacrifice.

    Threads Planted¶

    • "Porcelain?" in red ink Belle's final cryptic note in the Chernobyl notebook is the single word "Porcelain?" underlined in red. Its meaning is unexplained. Porcelain is the alias of Chezki Espinosa, leader of Red Calf, per the wiki index (Ch. 48).
    • The Auditors as a team Sam, Jordan, Spindle, and Miasma formally adopt the name "The Auditors" as their independent investigative unit, operating outside both the DVD and NSRA structures. Their future trajectory, membership, and legal standing are unresolved (Ch. 54).
    • Miasma framed for murder Miasma is accused of killing four NSRA security guards at Hatboro-Horsham. The Auditors believe the Kingdom framed him, but he remains a fugitive. His location and legal situation are unresolved (Chs. 5657).
    • Mr. ESP's rotating powers Mr. ESP possesses psychic abilities that change daily, making him unpredictable. His power on the day of the tunnel battle is "remote listening." The full scope and mechanics of this power rotation are unexplored (Chs. 57, 5960).
    • Kingdom recruiting Chernobyl The Kingdom of Keys attempted to recruit Chernobyl with money and documents for his family. Chernobyl violently rejected the offer, but the Kingdom's knowledge of his family creates leverage for future attempts (Chs. 5960).
    • Chernobyl's surrender and fate Chernobyl turns himself in to authorities on or around February 23, 2024, after his encounter with Sam. Whether he faces trial, is sent to Daedalus or Aurora Springs, or what happens to his family in Ukraine is entirely unresolved (Ch. 62).
    • Sam's radiation exposure aftermath Sam survived over 10 Grays of ionizing radiation through her regeneration. The long-term effects on her body, powers, and health are unknown. During her coma she shed approximately five pounds of skin. Her regeneration may have been permanently altered (Ch. 62).
    • Sam's PERKS threat level at 6/10 The updated assessment classifies Sam as "a superhuman of national interest" with tooth growth at G5 and regeneration at G6 (25-35x under severe injury). This classification attracts government scrutiny (SS.1).
    • Rachel Small's bipolar concern During Sam's coma, her mother Rachel reveals concerns about possible inherited bipolar disorder, suggesting a family mental health history that has not been previously disclosed (Ch. 62).
    • Aaron McKinley's blue flame Aaron develops a new toxic blue fire that produces poisonous fumes, damaging his own lungs in the process. He leaves Philadelphia on the vet's advice, positioning himself for a potential return as a more dangerous threat (AMK.2).
    • Aaron knows Sam's civilian identity Aaron identified Sam at Tacony Academy Charter High School and committed her face to memory. This knowledge persists even after his departure from Philadelphia (AMK.1).
    • Daisy Zhen's facility origin Daisy was kidnapped in Los Angeles at age eight and subjected to four years of experimentation by unnamed parties in a facility with barbed wire and locked doors. The identity of the organization, its location, and what they did to other children remain unknown (DZ.1).
    • Mrs. Z as Daisy's transfer agent Mrs. Z is the Kingdom operative who physically delivered Daisy from the facility to the Philly Phreaks in Philadelphia, connecting her weather-manipulation powers to the trafficking operation (DZ.1).
    • Agent Torres as sympathetic insider Torres evolves from an antagonistic NSRA agent to a whistleblower who provides classified information off-record and expresses doubts about his own agency. His future relationship with the Auditors and his professional fate are unresolved (Chs. 5859).
    • NSRA contesting Belle's will The NSRA is legally contesting Diane Williams's bequest of investigative materials to Sam. Laura Zhang is fighting the challenge, promising to tie them up in paperwork (Ch. 58).
    • Sam's dreaming of Diane During her coma, Sam experiences visions of Diane Williams in a flower-filled place. Whether these are meaningful visions or fever dreams is left ambiguous (Ch. 62).
    • "A nice man in a suit" Mrs. Westwood mentions that a "nice man in a suit" told her about "freaks coming and going" at the Music Hall, suggesting either Kingdom or NSRA surveillance directed someone to Jordan's mother to use her as a weapon against the Auditors (Ch. 54).
    • The Tacony Music Hall's legal status The Auditors cannot find the building's legal owner. Jordan has approximately $13,000 in savings. The question of whether they can legally secure their headquarters remains open (Ch. 59).

    Threads Progressed¶

    • Sam's evolving shark biology Sam's powers advance dramatically. She discovers tooth growth from her knuckles and wrists during torture (Ch. 52), and her regeneration is measured at 25-35x under severe injury (SS.1). Teeth grow passively from internal organs. Her body's "bite response" to penetrating trauma adds passive defenses. The PERKS reclassifies her tooth growth from a subcategory of "Shark-Like Biology" to a standalone G5 ability. Planted in Book 1, Ch. 14.
    • Sam's accelerating regeneration Now confirmed to scale proportionally with injury severity: 4x baseline for minor injuries up to 25-35x for severe injuries. She survives over 10 Grays of radiation a dose that has killed stronger superhumans. During her coma, her body sheds skin at an alarming rate. Planted in Book 1, Ch. 14; quantified in Book 3, Ch. 34.
    • Chernobyl's government energy deal The recording Sam made in Book 3 becomes the catalyst for the entire arc. She plays it for Miasma (Ch. 48) and Councilman Davis (Ch. 49), uses it to form the Auditors, and it drives the investigation into the NSRA. Planted in Book 3, Ch. 44.
    • The Kingdom of Keys The Kingdom expands its operations: Mr. Polygraph dispatches Aaron to kill Sam, the organization impersonates NSRA agents, frames Miasma for murder, attempts to recruit Chernobyl, and monitors the Auditors through surveillance devices and Mr. ESP's powers. New operatives identified: Mr. ESP (rotating psychic powers). Planted in Book 1, Ch. 12.
    • Sam's compromised civilian identity Aaron identifies Sam at her school (AMK.1) and uses this knowledge to ambush her. The Kingdom also knows where the Auditors operate from a listening device and Mrs. Westwood's information. Sam's civilian identity is now compromised to multiple threat vectors simultaneously. Planted in Book 1, Ch. 13.
    • Jordan's neglectful home life Escalates to its breaking point. Jordan's mother tracks them to the Music Hall, verbally abuses them, reveals their birth name "Avery," mocks their gender identity, and calls the police. Jordan permanently severs ties, with Mrs. Westwood declaring them "not her child anymore." Jordan reveals they gained their powers after being accidentally locked in a car trunk. Planted in Book 1, Ch. 11.
    • The dybbuk cycle Chernobyl accused Belle of being haunted by Franklin's ghost in Book 3. Sam now carries Belle's unfinished investigation, her detective equipment, and her letter. The arc asks whether Sam is continuing the dybbuk cycle or breaking it. Her choice to embrace rather than kill Chernobyl suggests an exorcism a break from inherited vengeance. Planted in Book 3, Ch. 44.
    • Deathgirl's trajectory Daisy participates in torturing Sam alongside Aaron (Chs. 5152), showing her continued weaponization. Her origin interlude (DZ.1) reveals her as a victim of kidnapping and systematic experimentation, complicating any simple villain reading. The Phreaks abandon Aaron after the failed attack (AMK.2), suggesting fractures in their alliance. Planted in Book 3, Chs. 3233.
    • Spindle's integration Connor has a new costume, trains with Playback, is a provisional Young Defenders member, and is dating Jordan. He co-founds the Auditors and fights in the subway tunnel battle. His transformation from Philly Phreaks defector to hero is well underway. Planted in Book 3, Chs. 3334, 39.
    • Belle's detective bequest The training and mentorship Belle provided in Book 3 pays off directly: Sam inherits the physical equipment, files, and investigative legacy. The bequest drives the entire arc's plot. Set up in Book 3 throughout; executed in Ch. 46.
    • Sam's blood-tracking technique Evolves from a simple combat trick into a sophisticated sensory mapping system. In the tunnel battle, Sam smears her blood across walls and floors to create a three-dimensional blood-sense map of her surroundings, coordinating her team's movements in total darkness (Ch. 60). Planted in Book 2, Ch. 26.
    • Mudslide's Kingdom career Mudslide appears as a full Kingdom operative in the subway tunnel ambush, trapping the Auditors in liquefied concrete. He retreats to help injured Mr. ESP rather than press the attack when Chernobyl arrives (Chs. 5960). Planted in Book 1, Ch. 13.
    • The Wolfcave / vigilante identity The Tacony Music Hall evolves from Jordan's personal hideout into the Auditors' formal headquarters, now equipped with Faraday cage, surveillance countermeasures, and government surplus equipment from Councilman Davis. Planted in Book 2, Ch. 20.
    • Kingdom shell companies Jordan's Book 3 research into Orion Holdings, Cerulean Dynamics, and Lockhart & Greene continues as the Auditors discover additional patterns linking shell companies to both the NSRA and the Kingdom, suggesting possible institutional overlap (Ch. 55). Planted in Book 3, Ch. 41.
    • Chernobyl's white blood Sam's blood sense detects Chernobyl's "white-hot" blood signature in the subway tunnels, confirming the unusual physiology she first observed in Book 3 (Chs. 5961). Planted in Book 3, Ch. 45.

    Threads Resolved¶

    • Chernobyl as an active threat Illya Myronovych Fedorov, who killed Professor Franklin and Liberty Belle, turns himself in to authorities after Sam's encounter with him in the subway tunnels. The immediate threat he posed to Philadelphia is neutralized, though his legal fate remains open. Planted in Book 1, Ch. 3 (Professor Franklin's killer identified in Book 3, Ch. 37); resolved in Ch. 62.
    • Sam's growing bloodlust Sam's internal struggle with her appetite for violence reaches its thematic resolution when she chooses compassion over killing. Given the opportunity to destroy Chernobyl with her teeth, she embraces him instead. The "evil voice" from Book 2 is answered by a conscious moral choice. Planted in Book 2, Ch. 21; resolved in Ch. 61.
    • Aaron McKinley and the Coyotes Aaron's arc as a local threat to Sam reaches its climax and resolution: he ambushes and tortures her but is mauled during her escape, abandoned by the Phreaks, and forced to leave Philadelphia with damaged lungs and a toxic new power. The Coyotes are confirmed dead as a gang; Aaron operates solo. Planted in Book 2, Chs. 2021; resolved in AMK.2.
    • The Philly Phreaks as a faction The remnant Phreaks (Pumice, Chrysalis, Deathgirl) ally with Aaron to attack Sam but abandon him afterward. Chrysalis tells Aaron he is "not worth the bullet." The Phreaks as a coherent group are effectively dissolved, with members scattering. Planted in Book 3, Chs. 3233; resolved in AMK.2.
