    Book 6: Sideshow¶

    A city on trial tears itself apart while a teenage hero discovers that the courtroom can be as brutal as any battlefield.

    Summary¶

    The arc opens with the end of Sam's freshman year and a summer defined by legal preparation and personal upheaval. Federal prosecutor Anne-Marie Gibson recruits Sam as the key witness in the upcoming trial of Illya Fedorov (Chernobyl), revealing that the NSRA deliberately obstructed contact between them for three months. Sam undergoes weeks of deposition preparation and a grueling mock cross-examination with defense attorney Jerry Caldwell, who probes her testimony for weaknesses. Meanwhile, Jamila breaks up with Sam, revealing that she may be asexual and cannot reconcile herself with the violence inherent in superhero life. Sam spirals into depression, showing up at Fury Forge's apartment at 1 AM and throwing herself into increasingly reckless late-night sparring sessions with Rampart.

    The Jump crisis escalates dramatically when Sam and Rampart discover a man undergoing a horrific mutagenic transformation in Love Park, his body erupting with uncontrollable metal shrapnel. Pumice of the Philly Phreaks attacks during the rescue, and Sam fights him in a brutal hand-to-hand engagement using her knuckle-spike teeth. Multiplex arrives with reinforcements and Pumice retreats on the back of Chimera, confirming the Phreaks' connection to the tainted Jump distribution. A full DVD briefing reveals four Jump incidents in eighteen hours across Philadelphia, and Sam shares intelligence linking the Phreaks to the drug crisis. Councilman Jamal Davis hints at Young Defenders graduations to the senior team, triggering a fraught meeting where Gale announces her desire to step back from active duty and Puppeteer discloses a psychological condition (NPD) that may disqualify her from advancement leaving Crossroads as the only viable candidate for promotion.

    The trial of Illya Fedorov begins August 15 and consumes nine days. Sam testifies on Day 4, presenting her video footage of Liberty Belle's final confrontation with Chernobyl. Under cross-examination, Caldwell introduces a handwritten note allegedly penned by Liberty Belle inviting Fedorov to the refinery where she died, which Sam reluctantly acknowledges resembles Belle's handwriting. A news article format covers Days 4 through 9, tracking the prosecution's case through forensic pathology, Professor Franklin's murder, hazardous materials charges, and the bombshell testimony of NSRA Agent Evelyn Shaw, who is exposed as having maintained a quid pro quo arrangement with Fedorov providing him resources in exchange for his radioactive abilities powering the Eastern Seaboard. The revelation ignites massive protests outside the courthouse.

    The Philly Phreaks exploit the chaos, launching a terrorist attack on the courthouse crowd by force-feeding protesters tainted Jump, causing mass mutagenic transformations. The DVD and Young Defenders split over whether to follow NSRA orders to suppress the protests, with Sam and Playback refusing while Rampart and Gossamer side with Multiplex. Sam undergoes a rage-fueled physical transformation denticle plates, bone blades from her elbows and shins, and recurved barbs lining her throat and fights Deathgirl in a savage one-on-one battle on the courthouse steps while Playback and Spindle, aided by Gale returning in disguise as a biker-helmeted stranger, take on Pumice. Inside the courthouse, Crossroads warns Illya that if Deathgirl copies his nuclear powers without a containment suit, she will kill everyone in Philadelphia; Illya stays put, praying in Hebrew for the safety of his daughter and Sam. Sam pins and restrains Deathgirl, who dissolves into sobbing and incoherent apologies. The heroes capture three of four Phreaks Deathgirl, Pumice, and Chrysalis while Chimera escapes. In the aftermath, Sam helps with search and rescue using her blood sense, receives a phone call from her father expressing pride in her for the first time, and attends a debriefing where the Phreaks are sent to Daedalus under chemical sedation.

    The arc concludes with multi-week recovery, during which the jury finds Fedorov guilty of manslaughter rather than murder. Crossroads graduates to the Delaware Valley Defenders, Rampart is named leader of the Young Defenders, and Captain Plasma relocates from Los Angeles to bolster the DVD's depleted ranks. Puppeteer and Playback resign Puppeteer to train as a paramedic, Playback to return to college, though Sam senses something deeper and bitter behind Playback's decision. Jamila moves away from Philadelphia entirely. Sam is invited to speak at a congressional legislative session. The Young Defenders are reduced to five members: Rampart, Gossamer, Blink, Spindle, and Bloodhound. Sam begins sophomore year on September 5, 2024, walking through metal detectors and past riot police into a city holding its breath.

    Themes¶

    • The theater of justice The trial is as much spectacle as legal proceeding. Sam is simultaneously witness, symbol, and pawn; the courtroom demands performance while claiming to seek truth. The arc interrogates whether institutional justice can address harms that institutions themselves enabled.
    • Attrition and isolation Sam loses Jamila (breakup), Gale (withdrawal), Puppeteer (resignation), Playback (resignation), and Crossroads (promotion) across a single arc. Each departure is rational and individual; collectively, they hollow out Sam's support network and push her toward the independent team she will eventually build.
    • Complicity and moral compromise The NSRA-Fedorov arrangement reveals that the system protecting the public was also protecting a murderer for its own benefit. The DVD's willingness to suppress protesters at NSRA orders mirrors the same institutional rot. Sam and Playback's refusal is principled but costly.
    • The body as battleground Sam's physical transformation during the courthouse fight (denticles, bone blades, throat barbs) represents rage made literal. Her injuries throughout the arc shrapnel wounds, broken ribs, a thigh puncture that takes weeks to close accumulate alongside emotional wounds that her regeneration cannot heal.
    • Sympathy for the condemned Sam's testimony is complicated by her genuine compassion for Illya, a man she hugged on a frozen battlefield. The manslaughter verdict (not murder) reflects the moral ambiguity she introduced to the narrative. Deathgirl's sobbing collapse after the fight briefly humanizes another "monster." The arc refuses to let its villains be simple.

    Threads Planted¶

    • Liberty Belle's handwritten note Caldwell introduces a note allegedly written by Belle inviting Fedorov to meet at the PES Refinery on the night of their final confrontation; Sam acknowledges the handwriting appears authentic; forensic confirmation is not established; if genuine, it reframes Belle's death as something she deliberately initiated (Ch. 95)
    • NSRA institutional corruption Agent Shaw's testimony exposes the NSRA's arrangement with Fedorov, sparking public outrage and protests; whistleblowers begin coming forward; congressional hearings and potential legislation are foreshadowed (Chs. 96, 100)
    • Sam's congressional invitation Sam is invited to speak at a legislative session in mid-October about superhuman regulation, establishing her as a political voice at the national level (Ch. 100)
    • Sam's evolving transformation Under extreme stress, Sam manifests denticle plates, inch-long bone blades from her elbows and shins, and recurved barbs lining her throat, representing a new and more extreme expression of her powers beyond the usual knuckle-spike teeth (Ch. 97)
    • Deathgirl's emotional breakdown Deathgirl dissolves into incoherent apologies and cries for her parents after being pinned, suggesting trauma and possible manipulation rather than pure villainy; her power to copy others' abilities makes her one of the most dangerous characters in the setting (Chs. 9798)
    • Deathgirl near Chernobyl Crossroads warns Illya that if Deathgirl copies his nuclear powers without a containment suit, she will cause a nuclear meltdown killing everyone in Philadelphia; the two must never come into contact (IF.4)
    • Illya Fedorov's daughter During the courthouse crisis, Illya prays for the protection of "our daughter," the first mention of him having a child; her identity, location, and caretaker are unknown (IF.4)
    • Crossroads and Illya's bond Crossroads and Illya share a moment of mutual understanding in the besieged courtroom, with Crossroads flipping a coin as a gesture of trust; this establishes an unusual hero-defendant connection (IF.4)
    • Gale's disguised return Jamila returns to fight Pumice disguised in biker leather and a motorcycle helmet, using her wind manipulation while pretending to be an unknown "telekinetic"; she leaves before Sam can identify her, contradicting her stated departure from heroism (DH.1)
    • Playback's hidden bitterness Playback's resignation is framed as a return to college, but Sam detects something deeper and unresolved; his whispered "Don't trust these bitches" to Sam before leaving suggests disillusionment with the DVD institutional structure (Ch. 100)
    • Chemical sedation of the Phreaks The captured Phreaks are chemically sedated and sent to Daedalus; Sam expresses discomfort with this, raising ethical questions about the treatment of metahuman prisoners (Ch. 99)
    • Chimera still at large Three of four Phreaks are captured, but Chimera (Elias Franklin) escapes; a news broadcast mentions a manhunt; he continues distributing tainted Jump (Chs. 99100)
    • Philadelphia crime wave and reinforcements The Jump crisis stretches Philadelphia's heroes to the breaking point; reinforcements arrive from New York, Wilmington, Baltimore, and D.C.; Captain Plasma relocates from Los Angeles; the city is described as a "powder keg" (Chs. 94, 100)
    • Kate Green as reporter A reporter named Kate Green covers the courthouse aftermath; this may be Kate Smith operating under a new identity after being declared officially dead (Ch. 99)
    • Young Defenders reduced to five With Crossroads promoted, Gale departed, and Puppeteer and Playback resigned, the YD is reduced to Rampart (leader), Gossamer, Blink, Spindle, and Bloodhound, with new recruitment a stated priority (Ch. 100)
    • Puppeteer's NPD and institutional discrimination Puppeteer's self-disclosure that her NPD diagnosis may bar her from advancement raises questions about ableism within superhero institutional structures; the disqualification is presented as systemic rather than individual (Ch. 92)
    • DVD leadership's recruitment discussion Fury Forge-led discussion identifies Crossroads, Rampart, Puppeteer, Playback, and others as graduation candidates; Multiplex proposes graduating Martinez and Reynolds with Harris, Li, and Chen close behind, establishing the pipeline for future team composition (BA.1)
    • Mrs. Xenograft's Kingdom recruitment The December 2021 flashback shows how Mrs. Zenith used coercion and unlimited research funding to recruit Dr. Trinh-Norwood, who later creates chimeric creatures and will eventually develop Hypeman (reverse-engineered Jump) and Vysera (a power suppressant); her acceptance marks the Kingdom's acquisition of a critical scientific asset (LTN.1)

    Threads Progressed¶

    • Chernobyl's trial (planted IF.3, Book 5) The trial proceeds across nine days; Sam testifies; the prosecution presents forensic evidence, expert witnesses, and video footage; Fedorov testifies in his own defense; the NSRA arrangement is exposed; the jury convicts on manslaughter rather than murder; sentencing is scheduled for September 28.
    • Sam and Jamila's relationship strain (planted Intermission 4.5, Ch. 65) The strain culminates in Jamila breaking up with Sam, citing possible asexuality and inability to handle the violence of hero life; Sam spirals into depression; by the arc's end Jamila has moved away entirely.
    • The Jump/Fly drug crisis (planted Book 5, Ch. 73) The crisis escalates dramatically with mutagenic Jump variants causing horrific transformations; four incidents occur in eighteen hours; the Phreaks weaponize tainted Jump as a tool of terrorism at the courthouse; the DEA emergency-schedules Jump as Schedule I.
    • The Philly Phreaks (planted Book 3) The Phreaks escalate from street-level crime to domestic terrorism, force-feeding tainted Jump to courthouse protesters; Deathgirl, Pumice, and Chrysalis are captured and sent to Daedalus; Chimera remains at large.
    • Sam's mental health and medication (planted Intermission 4.5, Ch. 64) Sam continues on lithium for bipolar disorder; Dr. Desai checks on her medication response; therapy sessions reveal her grief processing style (bottling followed by explosive release); she describes wanting to "rip skin off" in anger, suggesting the medication's emotional flattening effect is in tension with her raw emotional states.
    • Sam's recovery patterns and physical limits (planted Book 4) Sam takes injuries from Pumice (shrapnel, broken ribs, deep lacerations) that require weeks to heal even with regeneration; the thigh wound from Deathgirl takes weeks to close and is prone to infection; Dr. Abara notes Sam's tendency to reject and spit out sutures.
    • Kingdom of Keys operations (planted Book 2) The LTN.1 flashback shows the Kingdom's 2021 recruitment of a genetic engineer, expanding their scientific capabilities; this connects to later Kingdom drug development and the broader power struggle.
    • Young Defenders team composition (planted Book 1) The team undergoes dramatic restructuring: Gale steps back, Puppeteer and Playback resign, Crossroads graduates to the DVD, Rampart takes leadership, and the roster is reduced to five members with recruitment urgently needed.
    • Elias Franklin / Chimera at large (planted Book 5, Ch. 75) Chimera reappears carrying Pumice during the Love Park escape, confirming his alliance with the Phreaks; he remains free after the courthouse attack while three other Phreaks are captured.
    • Sam's unacknowledged role in Chernobyl's capture (planted Intermission 4.5) Sam's testimony makes her the public face of the prosecution's case, but her personal role in Illya's surrender remains largely unacknowledged; her conflicted feelings about condemning a man she showed compassion to complicate her testimony.
    • Spindle's Phreaks connections (planted Book 5, Ch. 77) Spindle corroborates intelligence on Deathgirl's instability during the DVD briefing, drawing on his prior relationship with the Phreaks; his knowledge continues to serve as a bridge between the heroes and their understanding of the Phreaks' internal dynamics.
    • Gale's departure from heroism (planted this arc, Ch. 88/92) Jamila announces her desire to leave the team and breaks up with Sam, but her disguised return to fight Pumice at the courthouse contradicts her stated withdrawal, suggesting she cannot fully abandon the life.
    • Ben Small's stance on Sam's heroism (planted Book 1) Ben calls Sam after the courthouse battle and expresses pride in her superhero work for the first time, marking a significant shift in the family dynamic around Sam's double life.

    • Sam's role as trial witness The question of whether Sam would testify, planted during Chernobyl's arraignment in IF.3 (Book 5), is resolved: she takes the stand on Day 4, presents her video evidence, survives cross-examination, and is excused (planted IF.3, resolved Chs. 9495)

    • Young Defenders graduation question The question of who would graduate to the senior team is resolved: Crossroads is the sole promotion; Rampart takes YD leadership; Puppeteer and Playback resign voluntarily (planted Ch. 91, resolved Ch. 100)
    • NSRA-Fedorov secret arrangement The long-suspected government complicity with Chernobyl is publicly exposed through Agent Shaw's testimony, confirming the NSRA provided Fedorov resources in exchange for his radioactive abilities powering the Eastern Seaboard (planted Book 4, resolved Ch. 96)
